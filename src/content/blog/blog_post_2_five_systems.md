# How Magic Works in Kazankor

Kazankor doesn't use the standard D&D spell system. No spell slots, no Wizard/Cleric/Druid spell lists, no Fireball. I removed all of it years ago and replaced it with five distinct magic systems, plus two further power sources.

There were two reasons for the swap. First, the spell-slot model didn't fit the world's premise — if magic is the raw material the gods are harvesting, treating it as a per-day resource that resets after a nap felt wrong. Second, having one magic system with subcategories produces characters that feel similar to each other. Different systems with different mechanical shapes produce characters that feel different at the table.

Here are the five.

**True Source.** Raw energy that destroys and remakes. The most powerful of the systems, and the rarest. Mortals can't channel it directly — they need a class structure called the Alchemist, which uses formulas and a Path/Tier progression to transform the body itself toward becoming True Source. This is the system the harvest is built around. Most practitioners never touch it.

**Tamga.** Runic inscription. The user inscribes patterns with Tamutash dust — the substance the harvest produces — and the patterns produce effects. Uses Effect Points as a resource. Tamga is offensive and defensive in equal measure; wards, shields, and barriers are core to the discipline, not edge cases. Anyone can learn it, given a teacher and patience.

**Songs of Creation.** Musical invocation. Every Song produces a real effect, and every Song has a built-in fault — an unintended consequence the singer cannot avoid. There is no Song without a price. Practitioners pass it down in person; you cannot learn it from a book.

**Dream Magic.** Access to a dream-dimension that has its own geography, inhabitants, and rules. Uses Dream Points. Practitioners enter the dream and act within it; what they do has consequences in the waking world.

**Outsource.** Borrowing power from entities that live on other planes through formal contracts and sacrifices. Each plane has its own contract structure and its own price. The borrower owes the entity; the entity owes the borrower; the contract is real and binding.

Beyond the five, there are two further power sources that aren't magic in the same sense but function as full systems. Sülde is elemental bending through pacts with elemental spirit-entities. Ruh Usta is a soul tradition that produces no detectable magic at all — its abilities pass through antimagic effects entirely.

The key design rule across all of these is no class gatekeeping. Anyone of any background can learn any system. The barriers are access and cost — finding a teacher, paying the price, putting in the time. A soldier can be a Tamga inscriber; a merchant can be an Outsource practitioner. The class doesn't lock the magic; the magic locks itself, by what it asks of you.
