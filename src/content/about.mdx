I'm Sarper Dundar, a technical artist based in Bursa, Turkey. I build Unity
tools, VFX assets, and shaders for indie developers — publishing under the
Masingan studio name on the Unity Asset Store. My focus is performance-first
work for low-poly and stylized games on URP.

I came to game development the long way around. From 2013 to 2024 I worked
professionally in dance — as an instructor, a competitive sportsman, and an
artist. Over those eleven years I taught students at every level, competed,
helped organize events at the schools I worked with, took on coordinator and
near-co-owner responsibilities when the situation called for it, and judged
at dance sport competitions our school ran. That stretch isn't tangential to
what I do now. Teaching for that long teaches you something specific about
breaking down complex things into the explanation that lands for _this_
person — the same instinct that shows up in mentoring junior artists,
writing documentation for tools other developers actually use, and shaping
shaders so the controls make sense to someone who didn't build them.

Through most of that period, games were the parallel thread. I'd been making
them since 2016 — jams, hobby projects, collaborations. The work was small
and the hours were stolen, but it never stopped. In 2024, after eight years
of running both careers, I decided to commit to the one I'd been building
toward the whole time.

The Asset Store work started shortly after. First the Searchlight System,
then Surface Text Placer, now the Wet Surfaces shader system. Each project
is picked to teach something that compounds into the next. The longer goal
is a technical artist role at a strategy game studio — the grand strategy
game project I'm contributing to as a side project is the portfolio piece
pointed at that path.

The dance years built the precision and the teaching habit. The game dev
years are where those are applied now.
