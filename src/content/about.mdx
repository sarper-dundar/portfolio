# About

I'm Sarper Dundar, a technical artist based in Bursa, Turkey. I build Unity
tools, VFX assets, and shaders for indie developers — publishing under the
Masingan studio name on the Unity Asset Store. My focus is performance-first
work for low-poly and stylized games on URP.

## The game dev thread

I've been making games in some form since 2016 — jams, hobby projects,
collaborations. For most of that time it ran alongside another career entirely.
The projects were small and the hours were stolen, but the work never stopped.

In 2024 I shifted focus fully to game development as a technical artist. The
Asset Store work started shortly after — first the Searchlight System, then
Surface Text Placer, then the Wet Surfaces shader system. Each project is
chosen to teach something that compounds into the next one.

## What I work with

Unity 6, URP 17.2, Shader Graph, HLSL, C# editor scripting, VFX Graph,
Substance Designer, Gaea.

The longer-term goal is a tech artist role at a strategy game studio. The
grand strategy map game I'm contributing to as a side project is the
portfolio piece for that path.

## The other eleven years

From 2013 to 2024 I worked as a professional dance instructor, dance artist,
and competitive dance sportsman. It was a real career, not a side job — over
the course of those years I taught students at all levels, competed, and took
on the kind of organizational work that comes with being embedded in a school
for long enough: coordinating schedules, managing relationships between
departments, stepping into near-coordinator and near-co-owner responsibilities
when the situation called for it. I also judged at dance sport events the
schools I worked with organized.

I mention this not as a curiosity but because it's where a large part of how
I work comes from. Teaching for that long — breaking down complex movement
for people at very different levels, finding the explanation that lands for
this specific student — is the same muscle I use when mentoring junior artists
or writing documentation that non-technical users can actually follow. Running
events and coordinating schools is project and people management, just in a
different domain. And competitive sport at any serious level teaches a kind
of precision about your own performance that doesn't really go away.

The 2024 shift wasn't an escape from something that wasn't working. It was
a decision, after eight years of doing both, to go all in on the thing I'd
been building toward the whole time.
